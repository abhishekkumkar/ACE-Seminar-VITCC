Speakers

Dr. Michael Cree

About Dr. Michael Cree

Dr Michael Cree is a senior lecturer in Electronic Engineering who specialises in machine vision including retinal imaging, 3D time-of-flight range imaging and biological approaches to visual odometry. Dr Cree has over 120 refereed journal and conference publications, and is co-editor of the text `Automated Image Detection of Retinal Pathology' published by CRC Press. He is currently involved in three major research projects funded by the Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment. Dr Cree is a member of ACPSEM,member of the ACM and a senior member of the IEEE.
Univeristy of Waikato
Title:Intelligent Machine Vision Technologies and Applications

Assoc Prof Nihal Kularatna

About Assoc Prof Nihal Kularatna

Assoc Prof Nihal Kularatna is an Associate Professor in the School of Engineering. He won the New Zealand Innovator of the Year Award (2013). His electronic engineering career spans 43 years and he is active in research in supercapacitor applications, power supply topologies, and power conditioning. He has authored 9 books and contributed to over 150 scholarly publications. His latest book titled Design of Transient Protection Systems will be released by Elsevier USA in Dec 2018. Multiple patents were granted for his supercapacitor assisted (SCA) circuit topologies. He is a Fellow of IENZ, Fellow of IET, and a Senior Member of IEEE. Before migrating to New Zealand, he was the CEO of the Arthur C Clarke Institute in Sri Lanka.

Dr Leandro Bolzoni

About Dr Leandro Bolzoni

Dr Leandro Bolzoni is a metallurgist, a mechanical engineer specialized in materials science by training, whose scientific interest is on the physical metallurgy of light metals (i.e. Ti, Al and Mg) and on advanced materials. Leandro joined the University of Waikato in July 2015 as senior lecturer and is the leader of the Titanium Research Group, which is central to the $14.5M MBIE-funded (Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment) Titanium Technologies New Zealand (TiTeNZ) project, a multi-disciplinary platform constituted by universities, Crown Research Institutes and industrial partners. Leandro is a committee member of the Society of Materials New Zealand Inc. (SMNZI) and received different prizes including the Charles-Hatchett Award from the Institute of Materials, Minerals and Mining (IOM3) in recognition of the significant contribution to metallurgical knowledge, and the potential of the results of the research to make a major technical, technological, commercial, environmental or other sustainability-related impact on important industrial sectors.

Dr Martin Atkins

About Dr Martin Atkins

Dr Martin Atkins is a Senior Lecturer in the School of Engineering at the University of Waikato. His general field of research is in energy systems engineering with a particular focus on developing and using Process Integration methodologies for optimising industrial energy systems and emissions reduction. He has collaborated in large research programmes examining energy and emission reduction potential in the diary and pulp and paper industries. He has close linkages with large industrial energy users and government agencies. He has published extensively in high impact journals.

Dr Vimal Kumar

About Dr Vimal Kumar

Dr Vimal Kumar is a lecturer in Computer Science who specialises in wireless sensors and security. He has previously worked on the development of secure wireless sensor networks and sensor clouds. His previous research has been funded by Florida Center for Cyber Security (FC2) and Cisco. Dr. Kumar leads the Cyber Security Laboratory (CROW) at the University of Waikato. Professor Siniah Ilanko

About Professor Siniah Ilanko

Professor Siniah Ilanko goes by his given name Ilanko. He does not have a famiy name but uses his father’s name Sinniah as his first name. Ilanko graduated from the University of Manchester (U.K) with a BSc in civil engineering and also obtained an MSc from the same university under the supervision of late Dr S.C. Tillman, investigating the effect of initial imperfections on in-plane loaded rectangular plates. He continued his doctoral studies at the University of Western Ontario under the supervision of Professor S.M. Dickinson, on the same topic. Since graduating, he has worked as a postdoctoral fellow (University of Western Ontario), lecturer, senior lecturer, associate professor (University of Canterbury), associate professor and currently a professor (University of Waikato). He has served as the Head of the Department of Mechanical Engineering (University of Canterbury, 2001-2002) and Chairperson and later the Head of School of Engineering (2013-2015). His research areas include vibration and stability of continuous systems, numerical modelling and adaptive mechanisms. His most recent research projects include adaptive stiffness foundations for earthquake isolation and crack detection using frequency measurements in structures with roving test bodies possessing rotary inertia. He has published 43 journal papers and in 2014 authored a book “The Rayleigh-Ritz Method for Structural Analysis”. He has secured two major grants, a Marsden grant for research into vibration analysis of complex structures and more recently a grant by the New Zealand government’s Ministry of Business Innovation and Employment (Category Smart Ideas) to conduct research on the development of an omnidirectional base isolator. He is a Subject Editor for Journal of Sound and Vibration, for analytical methods for linear vibration. He is also interested in computer-aided learning and has developed several interactive lectures and tutorials for teaching Mechanics of Materials and Vibration.
Univeristy of Waikato
Title:Structural Dynamics and Computational Mechanics Research

Dr. L. John Kennedy

About Dr. L. John Kennedy

Dr. L. John Kennedy is the Associate Professor in the Physics Division, at VIT Chennai. He received his UG and PG degree in Physics at Loyola college Chennai and obtained his doctoral degree from the Department of Environmental Technology, CSIR - Central Leather Research Institute (CLRI), Chennai. He underwent trainings at University de Alicante, Spain and Penn State University, Pensylvania, USA. He also worked as young scientist at the Department of Environmental Technology, CLRI, Adyar, Chennai. Dr. Kennedy has published 191 research publications in Scopus indexed international journals. His “H” index is 34. To his credit he has 9 patents. Many of his patents are commercialized in different industries in India and abroad. Dr. Kennedy is also the editorial board member of few international scopus indexed journals and periodic reviewer of many international journals. His areas of research include nano materials for catalysis, energy and environmental applications.

About Dr. G.VELMATHI

Dr. G.VELMATHI is the Professor in School of Electronics Engineering, at VIT Chennai. She received his UG from Thiagarajar college of engineering , PG from Madras Institute of Technology and obtained her doctoral degree from Indian Institute of Science (IISc) Bangalore.
